Zion National Park

Founded in 1919

Zion is known for its towering mountains and red canyons, which were carved out by the Virgin River. It's also known for popular (if difficult) hikes like Angels Landing and The Narrows.

  • $30 per private vehicle

Capitol Reef National Park

Founded in 1971

Located in the heart of Utah's red rock country, Capitol Reef is known for its cliffs and canyons in the Waterpocket Fold, which is essentially a wrinkle in the earth's crust.

  • $10 per private vehicle
Canyonlands National Park

Founded in 1964

True to its name, Canyonlands is famous for its canyons and buttes that have been carved out by the Colorado River (the same river that carved out the Grand Canyon). It's also known for its excellent mountain biking, with the 100-mile White Rim Road at Island in the Sky being the most popular trail.

  • $25 per private vehicle
Arches National Park

Founded in 1929

The sandstone arches, of course! The park has more than 2,000 natural stone arches, along with pinnacles, cliffs, and balanced rocks. The rocks are also a brilliant orange-red, which looks great in photos.

  • $25 per private vehicle
